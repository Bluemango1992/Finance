# PROTOCOL: Rule #1 Value Investing Methodology
**Source:** Phil Town 
**Objective:** Identify, value, and acquire publicly traded equities using private business valuation metrics to ensure capital preservation and target a minimum 15% annualized return.
**Core Rule:** Do not lose money.

---

## 🛑 Phase 1: Business Qualification (Qualitative Filter)
*Do not proceed to valuation until the business passes these criteria.*

- [ ] **The Comprehension Test:** Can you clearly explain how the business makes money and operates? (If no, discard the stock. Stop here.)
- [ ] **The Moat Test:** Does the business possess a durable protective barrier against competition (a "Moat")? 
- [ ] **The Predictability Test:** Because of the moat, is the company's growth rate sustainable and predictable for the next 10–20 years?

---

## 🧮 Phase 2: Intrinsic Value Calculation
*Use the following methods to determine what the business is actually worth today. Do not rely on standard Wall Street portfolio theory.*

### Valuation Protocol A: The "Ten Cap" Analysis (Primary Method)
*Use this method to evaluate the stock as if you were buying a private business entirely.*

1. **Calculate Owner Earnings:** 
   * `Gross Revenue - All Operating Expenses/CapEx = Owner Earnings` *(Also known as Free Cash Flow or Net Operating Income).*
2. **Apply the 10% Yield Rule:**
   * Calculate 10% of the total purchase price (Market Cap) of the company.
   * **The Protocol:** The current Owner Earnings must be equal to or greater than **10%** of the price you pay.
   * *Heuristic:* At this rate, the business will pay you back your initial investment in roughly 10 years purely from cash flow.

### Valuation Protocol B: Margin of Safety Analysis / DCF (Secondary Method)
*Use this method if projecting future growth is necessary, using a strict minimum acceptable rate of return.*

1. **Determine Historical Growth:** Analyze the past 10–20 years of growth. Throw out abnormal/anomaly years to find a realistic baseline.
2. **Project Future Value:** Estimate the cash flow 10 years into the future based on that predictable baseline.
3. **Apply the 15% Discount Rate:** 
   * Set your Minimum Acceptable Rate of Return to **15% per year**.
   * *The Shortcut Rule:* A 15% return doubles your money every 5 years. 
   * **The Protocol:** To find the present intrinsic value of a 10-year projection, take the projected future value per share and **divide it by 4** (cutting it in half twice).

---

## 🎯 Phase 3: The Purchase (Margin of Safety)
*Never pay full Intrinsic Value. The future is uncertain; the Margin of Safety protects against bad predictions.*

- [ ] **Calculate the Strike Price:** Multiply the Intrinsic Value (calculated in Phase 2) by **0.50**. This is your Maximum Buy Price.
- [ ] **Wait for a Market Event:** Exercise extreme patience. Do not buy until a macro-economic event or a temporary micro-economic panic pushes the stock price down to your 50% discount target.
- [ ] **Execute the Trade:** Buy the stock when the Margin of Safety is met.

> **Note on Capital Preservation:** By demanding a 50% discount on a great business with a moat, you heavily insulate your portfolio against a loss of capital over a 10-year horizon, even if your growth models are slightly wrong.

---

## 📡 Phase 4: Maintenance & Monitoring
*How to handle the asset once purchased.*

- [ ] **Monitor the "Story" Daily:** Track news and operations related to the fundamental reason you bought the company. 
- [ ] **Ignore Quarterly Noise:** Do not recalculate intrinsic value simply because of standard quarterly fluctuations.
- [ ] **Recalculation Trigger:** *Only* recalculate intrinsic value or consider exiting the position if the fundamental "story," the management structure, or the competitive Moat of the business permanently changes.
